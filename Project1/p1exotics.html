Exotics as Pets

One of the most appealing factors about exotic cats is their appearance but what many people don't realize is that exotic cats require special care and respect if someone wishes to keep one. That includes hunting and skill games for their predatory instincts and a large enclosure so they can stretch their legs. Since owning animals like these costs a lot of money most people can't afford them. It's always important to remember that these animals can never truely be pets even if they are tamed. The risks are always present.

African Serval

The Africal serval is a medium sized cat from Africa who is known to act like a dog. The serval loves water and has the ability to jump very high. Like all cats, the serval is a skilled hunter. It has long back legs which make it possible to catch birds nine feet in the air. They are able to dig a couple of feet in the ground to get under a fence. They require a special diet of meat and need a lot of exercise. Many people like the serval but wanted more of a house cat so the savannah cat was created and is much better for family life. Servals can bond with their humans but require patience. (Images 1, 2)

Cheetah

The Cheetah is a surprisingly good choice for an exotic cat. Most know the cheetah as one of the fastest land animals on the planet but dispite what many believe, the cheetah will only chase you if you run away from him due to his predatory instinct. They are actually considered the least dangerous of all the big cats. This does not mean you should let your children near this cat without supervision. This cat is naturally afraid of humans. In the wild they will flee if someone gets too close especially with direct eye contact. In captivity, cheetahs can live double their normal life expectantcy which is about 17 years. They require proper nutrition, plenty of space to run, and a special enclosure. (Images 3, 4)

Caracal

Similar in appearance to the Lynx, the Caracal has a personality similar to the Serval. They originate from parts of Africa and Asia. This cat eats about 2-3lbs of meat every day and can live around 17 years. They are generallyvery playful and friendly to other people and animals. They are intelligent and curious in nature. The carcal as a pet has been around for centuries. Carcals are able to jump high and need a special enclosure. They require walks on leashes and raw meat as their diet. (Images 5, 6)

Ocelot

The Ocelot is a medium sized cat from South America. They have spotted coats and large eyes. This cat loves hunting and fishing and is most active at dusk or dawn. The Ocelot likes to spend most of his time hunting and for that reason is not good with other pets or small children. They can be noisy and like to rub their scent on many different objects. Caring for an Ocelot is similar to other exotic cats. They require outdoor space, special foods and play time. They can be affectionate and need a lot of socialization. (Images 7, 8)
